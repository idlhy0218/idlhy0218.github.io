Statistics for Sociologists - Spring 2023
University at Albany, SUNY | Co-Teacher

This course introduces students to statistical methods and data analysis techniques commonly used in sociological research. Students learn how to analyze social data using statistical software, interpret results, and present findings effectively.

My teaching philosophy is grounded in creating an inclusive, engaging, and intellectually rigorous learning environment. I believe that effective teaching in sociology requires not only conveying theoretical knowledge and methodological skills but also fostering critical thinking about social structures and inequalities.

I strive to make sociological concepts accessible by connecting abstract theories to students' lived experiences and contemporary social issues. By demonstrating the relevance of sociological perspectives to everyday life, I help students develop a deeper understanding of how social structures shape individual experiences.
