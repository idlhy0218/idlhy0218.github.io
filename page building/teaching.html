Medical Sociology
Semesters: Spring 2026, Fall 2025, Spring 2025, Fall 2024, Summer 2024

This course introduces students to sociological perspectives on health, illness, and healthcare systems. We examine how social structures, inequalities (race, class, gender), and institutional practices influence both individual and population health outcomes, as well as the historical and contemporary operation of medical institutions in the United States.

Criminology
Semesters: Spring 2026, Fall 2025, Summer 2025

This course provides a comprehensive sociological analysis of crime, deviance, and the criminal justice apparatus. Students examine foundational theoretical frameworks explaining criminal behaviors, analyze spatial and demographic patterns of crime, and critically evaluate the roles of police, courts, and correctional systems in shaping public safety and inequality.
