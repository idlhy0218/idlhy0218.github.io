About Me

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Sociology at the University at Albany, SUNY. My research focuses on population health, criminology, and life-course analysis, with particular emphasis on spatial methods and longitudinal analysis.

Population Health

Investigating temporal and spatial dimensions of health inequalities.

Criminology

Studying adolescent behavior, sexual violence, and the effects of police interactions on youth development.

Methods

Specializing in spatial analysis, longitudinal methods, and advanced statistical techniques using R, STATA, and ArcGIS.

Research Interests

  • Population Health: Health disparities, Mental health, Spatial analysis of health outcomes
  • Criminology: Adolescent behavior, Sexual violence, Police interactions
  • Life-course Analysis: Neighborhood effects, Structural inequalities
  • Methods: Spatial analysis, Longitudinal analysis, Advanced statistical techniques
